'Ilm al-huruf

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search
Arabic numerological process

Ilm al-urf (Arabic: ) or the science of letters, also called Islamic lettrism, is a process of Arabic numerology whereby numerical values assigned to Arabic letters are added up to provide total values for words in the Quran, similar to Hebrew gematria. Used to infer meanings and reveal secret or hidden messages.

Etymology[edit]

Ilm al-urf is composed of the two words ilm (Arabic: ) meaning "knowledge", and urf (Arabic: ), the plural of the word arf (Arabic: ), meaning "letters".
